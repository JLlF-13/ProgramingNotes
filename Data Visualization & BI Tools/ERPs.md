
An ERP is a business management system that helps companies plan, organize, and automate most of their internal processes (finance, sales, purchasing, logistics, production, accounting, etc.). Its main goal is to centralize all company data in one place, avoiding duplicates and making daily work easier.

### Modular Structure of ERPs

ERP systems work in a modular way:

- Each module corresponds to a specific area of the company (sales, production, inventory, finance, etc.).
    
- All modules are connected and share information with each other.

---

### Integration

All company processes are connected. Data is entered only once and shared through a central database. This improves efficiency, saving time and reducing costs.

### Modularity

The ERP is divided into modules (sales, purchasing, accounting, inventory, etc.). Each module has its own function, but all share information. This allows the system to be adapted and customized.

### Adaptability

ERP systems can adjust to the specific needs of each company, regardless of its size, industry, or way of working—thanks to flexible configuration, industry-specific modules, customization, and scalability.


---

### The Good

- It adapts to the business needs
    
- Keeps information updated in real time
    
- Deletes duplicated data and avoids errors
    
- Integrates all departments (sales, purchases, finance, HR, etc.) into one system
    
- Everyone works with the same database, improving access to information
    
- Increases efficiency and helps reduce costs
    
- Improves relationships with customers and suppliers thanks to centralized data
    
- Offers better control of business activity and supports decision-making
    
- User and role management: each employee only sees what they need (traceability)


### The Bad

- High cost: requires a big investment of time and money
    
- Complex implementation: needs planning and changes in company processes
    
- Resistance to change: employees may struggle to adapt
    
- Training required: staff must learn how to use the system properly
    
- Integration problems with some external programs
    
- High dependency on the supplier for updates, support, and customization
    
- Technical risks: failures or misconfigurations can affect the whole company

---

### ERP-CRM-BL Systems Integrated

Nowadays, CRM and BL are natural complements of the ERP systems; the majority of ERP now have CRM and BL integrated.

Additionally, ERP has modular plugins that let CRM and BL tools expand in very different ways

For this, the majority of integrated management systems are simply called ERP systems and include parts from ERP, CRM, and BL
