NoSQL databases come in different models, each optimized for specific types of data and use cases. Here's a breakdown of the most common types:

|Type|Description|Example Use Case|Popular Systems|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Document**|Stores data as JSON-like documents|User profiles, product data|MongoDB, CouchDB|
|**Key-Value**|Simple key-value pairs|Caching, session storage|Redis, DynamoDB|
|**Column-based**|Stores data in columns instead of rows|Analytics, time-series data|Cassandra, HBase|
|**Graph**|Stores nodes and relationships|Social networks, recommendation engines|Neo4j, ArangoDB|
### Notes

- **Document databases** are great when your data has nested structures or varies between records.
    
- **Key-value stores** are ultra-fast and ideal for simple lookups.
    
- **Column-based systems** are built for massive datasets and analytical queries.
    
- **Graph databases** shine when relationships between entities are the core of your data.
